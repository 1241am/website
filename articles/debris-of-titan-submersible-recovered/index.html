The Titan submersible was carrying five passengers when it lost communication two hours into its dive to explore the remains of the Titanic. After an international search-and-rescue effort, the vessel was presumed lost and its passengers dead. On Wednesday, debris from the Titan was returned to land off Newfoundland, including the 22-foot hull, crinkled and twisted with exposed wires and cables. The debris will be taken to a U.S. port for further analysis and testing, and medical professionals will conduct a formal analysis of any presumed human remains. The U.S. Coast Guard is leading the investigation into why the submersible imploded and has convened a marine board of investigation. It is working with other national and international agencies and the investigation could take 18 to 24 months. The recovered debris could contain vital information about what happened to the Titan and investigators will be looking for a point of failure of the hull, how pieces of carbon fiber and titanium were connected, and if any electronic data is recoverable.
