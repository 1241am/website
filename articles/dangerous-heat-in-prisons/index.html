The record June heat has been particularly dangerous inside the state’s prisons in Texas, where indoor temperatures can top 110 degrees and air-conditioning is not provided in most cases. Inmates have been struggling to survive in the extreme heat, relying on warm water, wet towels and fans to cool off. Desperate for help, some inmates have resorted to screaming in unison for water or for help with an inmate who had passed out. The Republican-controlled House proposed spending $545 million to install air-conditioning in the majority of state prisons that do not have it, but the bill to require cooling died in the State Senate. Despite a record surplus, the final state budget did not include money specifically for prison air-conditioning. The lack of action has been blamed on Lt. Gov. Dan Patrick, a conservative Republican who leads the Senate. The truth is the state is paying millions of dollars a year in heat-related lawsuits and the need for cooling facilities is becoming increasingly urgent.
