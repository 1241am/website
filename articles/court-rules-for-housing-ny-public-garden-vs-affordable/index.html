Court Rules for Housing: NY Public Garden vs. Affordable

June 28, 2023

The Elizabeth Street Garden in Manhattan is a popular open space that has been a source of contention for more than 10 years. Residents have been fighting to keep the garden, while the city has been pushing for the development of affordable housing on the site. On Tuesday, the housing plan prevailed when an appellate court cleared the way for a 123-apartment development for lower-income people over the age of 62 and their families. The ruling was a “huge win” for the city’s housing commissioner, who noted that the fight highlights how difficult it can be to build affordable housing in wealthy areas. The garden’s supporters are disappointed with the ruling and are seeking permission to appeal the case to the state’s highest court. They are hoping for a solution that preserves the garden while still providing housing. The project is part of the city’s effort to address its dire housing shortage, and the opposition to it is emblematic of the “not-in-my-backyard” mentality of many higher-income areas. Environmental regulations have also been used to fight the development of new housing. The court’s decision is a step towards providing much needed affordable housing in New York City.
